Draft a comprehensive Letter of Intent for a software engineering engagement informed by the Expression of Interest, engagement profile, recommendation, and project plan.

The letter should be addressed to the scientific principal investigator and focus on how the software engineering team will support their project.

Use GitHub-flavored markdown and include the following sections:

- **Date** pre-populated with $today.
- **To / From** block naming the PI and the software engineering lead; use placeholders if missing.
- **Executive Summary** describing the purpose of the engagement.
- **Review Context** briefly reflecting the EOI assessment: scope clarity, feasibility, readiness, expected impact, and any follow-up questions that condition the engagement.
- **Engagement Objectives** with 3-5 bullet points tied to outcomes.
- **Proposed Approach & Timeline** broken into 3-4 phases totaling three to six months, each with goals, expected outputs, and indicative duration.
- **Deliverables & Success Measures** outlining tangible artifacts and how success will be evaluated.
- **Skills & Resourcing Approach** describing the core capabilities required and how the team expects to source them without overcommitting named individuals.
- **Collaboration Model** detailing team composition, PI coordination, communication cadence, time-zone considerations, and governance.
- **Dependencies & Assumptions** capturing data access, tooling, staffing, and stakeholder commitments.
- **Risk Register** listing the top risks with mitigation strategies.
- **Scientific Outcomes & Follow-On Opportunities** describing expected scientific impact and proposal/funding paths to explore.
- **Next Steps** providing a short list of actions to move toward kickoff.
- **Closing & Signature** suitable for inclusion in a formal letter.

Embed relevant details from the engagement profile, recommendation, and project plan.
If any critical information is missing, gracefully note assumptions or placeholders.
Do not invent budget figures unless explicitly provided.
Do not name active funding calls unless the profile or EoI names them.
Do not name specific staff members unless provided by the EoI or team context.
Ensure the tone remains supportive and aligned with collaborative scientific work.
