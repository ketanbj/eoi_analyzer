Create a project plan for the recommended engagement. Use GitHub-flavored markdown and include these sections:

- **Plan Overview** summarizing the engagement goal and planning assumptions.
- **Rubric-Informed Scope and Readiness** explaining how the project plan responds to the EOI scope, feasibility, impact, and readiness assessment.
- **Phase Plan** with 3-5 phases. For each phase include objective, duration, main activities, outputs, owner roles, and exit criteria.
- **Milestones** with concrete checkpoints and expected evidence of progress.
- **Deliverables** listing tangible artifacts the team should produce.
- **Skills & Staffing Plan** listing each required skill, why it matters, who likely provides it, how the project can obtain it, and whether it is needed during discovery, build, hardening, handoff, or proposal follow-up.
- **Team & Collaboration Model** covering roles, cadence, decision-making, PI geography, time-zone constraints, and stakeholder inputs.
- **Technical Approach** covering architecture, data, tooling, testing, deployment, performance, documentation, and reproducibility where applicable.
- **Scientific Outcome Path** explaining how engineering deliverables enable science outputs.
- **Proposal/Funding Follow-Up Path** listing realistic categories and next validation steps.
- **Success Measures** with measurable outcomes or acceptance criteria.
- **Risk Register** with risk, impact, likelihood, mitigation, and owner.
- **Immediate Next Steps** for the first two weeks.

When thinking about skills, include both delivery skills and enabling skills. Examples: software engineering, research software engineering, architecture, product/program management, data engineering, AI/ML, MLOps, HPC/performance, cloud/infrastructure, security/privacy, UI/UX, technical writing, documentation, testing/QA, open-source governance, community support, training, proposal development, and domain-science liaison.

For "how to get the skill", consider GT CSSE/VISS staff, PI-team expertise, students or trainees, reusable assets from past projects, short advisory consults, internal GT partners, external collaborators, vendor/cloud support, targeted workshops, and future proposal-funded hires or contracts.

Keep the plan concrete and executable. Use assumptions where needed, but label them clearly.
Do not invent budget figures or named personnel unless provided.
